PORTFOLIO

This webpage is a collection of the projects I have been working on from 2013 to today.
The project topics are broad, including art, coding, science and typesetting.
Most of the files are available in the associated github-repository: https://github.com/d-sacre/portfolio.
If they are stored on an external server (e. g. a server of a publisher), I will provide the link to the original source.

ART

Last Update: January 19, 2022

My interest for art originates during my studies out of necessity: Every publication requires some custom graphics. Usually, whilst working on scientific publications, there is no graphics designer available. So it became necessary to learn some basic skills in photography, color theory, format conversion, post-processing and 3D modeling to be able to publish scientifically sound and visually appealing papers. This occupation with art for professional reasons started to spark also personal interest; especially with procedural art generation by code execution.

CODING

Last Update: January 18, 2022

Similar to my interest in art, I started coding for professional reasons: In research, one often times needs to measure quantities for which no commercial instrument is available. Custom built or modified equipment always requires coding skill to get it up and running. If it is working, one could need to deal with huge amounts of data, which can be hard to analyze with the GUI programs on the market. As soon as simulations are required, there is no way around of being proficient in at least one programming language.

TYPESETTING

Last Update: January 20, 2022

My first contact with professional (scientific) typesetting was 2013. During my studies, I had to write many protocols and two theses. The scientific notation requirements made LaTeX basically a necessity. After tinkering a bit on my own, I attended two university courses and received proper training (mostly LaTeX2ε). This led to an ongoing obsession with LaTeX, especially creating images with TikZ and visualizing data with pgfplotstable. Over the years, I was involved in typesetting four scientific papers and assisted countless times if colleagues or friends had LaTeX problems. Most recently, I began to explore LuaLaTeX and LaTeX3 with its seemingly endless realm of possibilities. Additionally, I started to create my first homemade usepackages and classes.
