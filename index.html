ART

Last Update: January 24, 2022

My interest in art stems from my time as a student and was originally acquired out of necessity: In every scientific publication some graphics need to be custom-created, but the fewest scientists have professional graphics design training. In order to be able to publish not only scientifically sound, but also visually appealing and easy-to-read papers, learning some basic skills in photography, color theory, format conversion, post-processing and 3D modeling became necessary. From there, my relationship with art grew from professional necessity to personal passion. I especially love the concept of procedural art, since it combines the seemingly contradictory scientific rationality with the artistic ease in a very beautiful way.

CODING

Last Update: February 3, 2022

Similar to my interest in art, I originally started coding for professional reasons: In research, one often times needs to measure quantities without having the option to use commercially available instruments. However, custom-built or modified equipment always requires coding-skills in order to get it up and running.
Once the hardware is set up and working, one needs to effectively analyze and sometimes deal with huge amounts of data. This is often hard or even next to impossible with the GUI-programs which are available on the market.
Another benefit of being proficient in at least one programming language: If computer simulations are required, one has a basic understanding of the underlying programming concepts, which makes the whole simulation design phase a lot easier, no matter which language or tool is used.

TYPESETTING

Last Update: June 21, 2022

My first contact with professional, scientific typesetting was 2013. During my studies, I had to write many protocols and two theses. Their requirements regarding scientific notation made LaTeX basically a necessity.
After tinkering a bit on my own, I attended two university courses and received proper training in (mostly) LaTeX2ε. This led to an ongoing obsession with LaTeX, especially creating images with TikZ and visualizing data with pgfplotstable. Over the years, I was involved in typesetting four scientific papers and assisted countless times if colleagues or friends had LaTeX problems. Recently, I began to explore LuaLaTeX and LaTeX3 with its seemingly endless realm of possibilities. As a result of the exploration, I started to create my first homemade packages and classes.
