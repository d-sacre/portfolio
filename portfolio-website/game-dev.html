Mission Calypso: A html/ccs/JavaScript game as a university project

Last Update: February 3, 2022

During the university IT training, I participated in a course about html/css/JavaScript. For the final course assignment, I teamed up with three other students to create a real-time resource managment game situated in the fictious Victorian Space Age. Over the course of approximately six weeks, we created a feature complete minimal viable product, which we decided to name "Mission Calypso", after Saturn's moon where we set the background plot.
The player is acting as Commander Been, who has been tasked by Captain Errol to retrieve vital resources from outer space to ensure the progress of the British Empire. Copper ore is the main interest, which is desperately needed for further electrifying the home world. Aside from that, the player needs to gather additional minerals, which help to manage the energy and oxygen consumption. When the amount of copper ore required to complete the quest is collected, it is possible to continue mining. However, gathering more or work faster is not always better: More mining means more wear on the rocket, less energy and oxygen reserve as well as a higher take-off weight. All these factors determine the probability whether the take-off is successfull and the player returns to the earth safely, or if the rocket explodes on take-off.

The final version of the project is available here. This university project includes assets which the team has written permission to use for the educational purpose of the project only.
